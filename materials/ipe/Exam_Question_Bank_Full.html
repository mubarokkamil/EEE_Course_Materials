PART B — TRUE / FALSE QUESTIONS

Management Functions

T/F 1. An organization is just a building where employees work. → FALSE. An organization is a group of people working together in a structured way to achieve a goal.

T/F 2. Management is the process of converting inputs (resources) into outputs (goals). → TRUE.

T/F 3. There are 5 categories of management resources. → FALSE. There are 7 categories: People, Money, Time, Work Procedures, Energy, Materials, Equipment.

T/F 4. Efficiency means doing the right thing. → FALSE. Efficiency means doing things right (using resources productively). Effectiveness means doing the right thing.

T/F 5. A company can be highly efficient but still fail if it is pursuing the wrong goal. → TRUE. (High efficiency, low effectiveness = "The Smart Fool" scenario.)

PART C — ONE-LINE SHORT QUESTIONS & ANSWERS

Management Functions

Q1. What is an organization? A: A group of two or more people working together in a structured way to achieve a specific goal.

Q2. Define Management. A: The process of using available resources (inputs) to achieve organizational goals (outputs) efficiently and effectively.

Q3. List the 7 categories of management resources. A: People, Money, Time, Work Procedures, Energy, Materials, Equipment.

Q4. What is Efficiency? A: A measure of how well or productively resources are used to achieve a goal — "doing things right."

Q5. What is Effectiveness? A: A measure of how appropriate the goals are and the degree to which they are achieved — "doing the right thing."

Q6. What is Organizational Performance? A: A measure of how efficiently and effectively managers use organizational resources to satisfy customers and achieve goals.

Q7. List the 5 functions of management (traditional view). A: Planning, Organizing, Staffing, Directing (Leading), Controlling.

Q8. What are the 4 functions of management according to Henri Fayol? A: Planning, Organizing, Leading, Controlling.

Q9. Define Planning. A: Identifying and selecting appropriate goals and courses of action for the organization — deciding what to do, how to do it, and how to allocate resources.

Q10. Define Organizing. A: Structuring working relationships in a way that allows organizational members to work together to achieve goals.

Q11. Define Leading. A: Articulating a clear vision and energizing organizational members using power, influence, persuasion, and communication to achieve organizational goals.

Q12. Define Controlling. A: Evaluating how well an organization is achieving its goals, monitoring performance, and taking corrective action to improve performance.

Q13. What is the outcome of the Leading function? A: Highly motivated and committed organizational members.

Q14. What is the outcome of the Controlling function? A: The ability to measure performance accurately and regulate the organization for efficiency and effectiveness.

Q15. What are the three types of managers? A: First-Line Managers, Middle Managers, and Top Managers.

Q16. What do First-Line Managers focus on? A: Day-to-day operations — supervising non-managerial employees who perform the actual work.

Q17. What do Middle Managers do? A: Supervise first-line managers and find the best way to use departmental resources to achieve company goals.

Q18. What do Top Managers do? A: Set long-term goals, monitor the entire organization, and form the top management team (CEO, COO, etc.).

Q19. Name the three categories of managerial roles identified by Henry Mintzberg. A: Decisional, Informational, and Interpersonal roles.

Q20. Name the four Decisional roles. A: Entrepreneur, Disturbance Handler, Resource Allocator, Negotiator.

Q21. Name the three Informational roles. A: Monitor, Disseminator, Spokesperson.

Q22. Name the three Interpersonal roles. A: Figurehead, Leader, Liaison.

Q23. What is the difference between Disseminator and Spokesperson? A: Disseminator transmits information inward (to employees). Spokesperson transmits information outward (to the public, government, or press).

Q24. What are the four building blocks of Competitive Advantage? A: Efficiency, Quality, Innovation, Responsiveness to Customers.

Q25. Is management an art or a science? Answer in one sentence. A: Management is both an art (depends on personal creativity and judgment) and a science (based on proven principles, models, and data-driven analysis).

Q26. What are the 5 challenges for management in a global environment? A: Increasing global organizations, building competitive advantage, maintaining ethical standards, managing diverse workforce, and utilizing IT and e-commerce.

Evolution of Management Theory

Q27. What is the key lesson from Sun Tzu's "The Art of War" for management? A: The marketplace is a battlefield — managers must develop competitive strategy, conserve resources, and outmaneuver rivals.

Q28. What concept did Adam Smith introduce that is foundational to modern management? A: Division of Labor — breaking complex tasks into smaller, specialized parts assigned to different workers.

Q29. What is Scientific Management and who developed it? A: Developed by Frederick W. Taylor; it seeks to increase productivity by scientifically studying work and finding the "one best way" to perform each task.

Q30. What research method did Taylor use to find the most efficient work procedures? A: Time and Motion Studies — observing and timing each movement in a task to eliminate waste.

Q31. State Taylor's four principles of Scientific Management. A: (1) Scientifically study each task. (2) Select and train workers in the best method. (3) Cooperate with workers to ensure use of the method. (4) Divide work between management (planning) and workers (execution).

Q32. What did Max Weber contribute to management theory? A: The concept of an ideal Bureaucracy — a rational, rule-based organizational structure with formal hierarchy, merit-based promotion, specialization, and impersonality.

Q33. Name any five of Henri Fayol's 14 principles. A: Division of Work, Authority and Responsibility, Discipline, Unity of Command, Unity of Direction, Scalar Chain, Remuneration, Equity, Esprit de Corps (and others).

Q34. What is the Hawthorne Effect? A: Workers perform better simply because they know they are being observed — the act of attention itself improves performance.

Q35. Who conducted the Hawthorne Studies? A: Elton Mayo.

Q36. What is the key difference between Theory X and Theory Y? A: Theory X assumes employees dislike work and need strict control; Theory Y assumes employees are self-motivated, responsible, and creative when conditions are right.

Q37. What is Synergy in Systems Theory? A: The whole is greater than the sum of its parts (1 + 1 > 2) — subsystems working together produce more than each would produce independently.

Q38. What is Equifinality? A: Different starting conditions or paths can lead to the same final outcome.

Q39. What does Contingency Theory state? A: There is no single "one best way" to manage an organization — the most effective approach depends on the specific situation (contingencies).

Q40. What is TQM? A: Total Quality Management — an organization-wide approach where all employees and units work harmoniously to satisfy customers through continuous quality improvement.

Q41. What is Kaizen? A: A Japanese concept meaning continuous, incremental improvement — central to TQM.

Q42. What is a Learning Organization? A: An organization that continuously anticipates and adapts to change faster than competitors through experimental and external learning.

Q43. Name the two ways a Learning Organization learns. A: Experimental Learning (learning from internal trials and experience) and External Learning (learning from competitors, customers, and benchmarking).

Organization Structure

Q44. Define Organizational Structure. A: The formal arrangement of jobs within an organization — how activities are divided, organized, and coordinated.

Q45. What are the four factors that determine organizational design? A: Environment, Strategy, Technology, and Human Resources.

Q46. What is Work Specialization? A: The degree to which tasks are divided into separate jobs, each completed by a different person.

Q47. What are "human diseconomies"? A: Negative effects on workers from over-specialization — including boredom, fatigue, stress, poor quality, high absenteeism, and increased turnover.

Q48. What is Departmentalization? A: The basis by which jobs are grouped together into departments — can be functional, product-based, geographic, process-based, or customer-based.

Q49. What is Chain of Command? A: The continuous line of authority that extends from the top of the organization to its lowest level, clarifying who reports to whom.

Q50. What is Span of Control? A: The number of subordinates a manager can effectively supervise.

Q51. What is the difference between Centralization and Decentralization? A: Centralization concentrates decision-making at the top levels; Decentralization pushes decision-making authority down to lower-level managers.

Q52. What is Formalization? A: The degree to which jobs within the organization are standardized and employee behavior is guided by explicit rules and procedures.

Q53. Distinguish between Mechanistic and Organic structures. A: Mechanistic = high specialization, rigid hierarchy, heavy formalization — suitable for stable environments. Organic = flexible roles, open communication, low formalization — suitable for dynamic environments.

Q54. What is a Simple Structure? A: A flat, informal structure with low specialization, few formal rules, and centralized authority held by the owner/founder. Best for small organizations.

Q55. What is the main weakness of a Functional Structure? A: Poor cross-departmental communication — "functional silos" where departments work in isolation rather than collaborating.

Q56. What is a Divisional Structure? A: An organizational design where the company is divided into self-contained divisions, each organized around a specific product, geographic region, or customer type.

Q57. What is a Matrix Structure? A: A structure that simultaneously groups employees by function AND by product/project teams — resulting in each employee having two bosses.

Q58. Which Fayol principle does the Matrix Structure violate? A: Unity of Command.

Q59. What is a Virtual Organization? A: A small core of full-time employees that temporarily hires specialists as needed — the company coordinates rather than employs a large permanent workforce.

Q60. Distinguish between Virtual, Network, and Modular organizations. A: Virtual = small core that hires specialists temporarily. Network = small core that outsources major functions (e.g., Apple outsourcing manufacturing). Modular = manufacturer that outsources components and does final assembly in-house.

Q61. What is Task Variety? A: The number of new and different problems a manager or worker encounters in their job.

Q62. What is Task Analyzability? A: The degree to which standard, pre-established solutions are available to handle problems encountered in a job.

Personnel Management / HRM

Q63. Define HRM according to the British Institute of Personnel Management. A: "That part of management which is concerned with people at work and with their relationships within an organization."

Q64. State the four operative functions of HRM according to the American Management Association. A: Procuring, Developing, Maintaining, and Utilizing a workforce.

Q65. Name five objectives of HRM. A: Attract/develop/retain talent; optimum utilization of human resources; encourage best employee contribution; build management-worker trust; increase worker efficiency through training.

Q66. Name the five levels of Maslow's Need Hierarchy in order from lowest to highest. A: Physiological → Safety → Social/Belongingness → Esteem → Self-Actualization.

Q67. What is Intrinsic Motivation? A: Motivation that comes from internal satisfaction — the work itself is rewarding.

Q68. What is Extrinsic Motivation? A: Motivation driven by external rewards — salary, bonuses, awards, promotions.

Q69. State Herzberg's key finding in one sentence. A: Removing dissatisfaction (through hygiene factors) does NOT create motivation — you must also provide motivators (achievement, recognition, growth) to truly motivate employees.

Q70. Name four Hygiene Factors (Herzberg). A: Salary, Job Security, Working Conditions, Company Policy, Quality of Supervision.

Q71. Name four Motivators (Herzberg). A: Achievement, Recognition, Responsibility, Growth, Interesting Work.

Q72. State the Expectancy Theory formula. A: Motivation = Expectancy × Instrumentality × Valence.

Q73. What does Valence mean in Expectancy Theory? A: The degree to which the employee values the reward being offered.

Q74. What is the defense mechanism called "Projection"? A: Attributing your own faults, mistakes, or feelings to other people.

Q75. What is the defense mechanism called "Denial"? A: Refusing to accept an unpleasant reality.

Q76. What is Job Satisfaction? A: The degree to which an individual feels positively or negatively about various aspects of their job.

Q77. What is Job Performance? A: The quantity and quality of tasks accomplished by an individual or group at work.

Q78. Define Job Analysis. A: A formal, scientific, and systematic study of a job to collect all pertinent facts about what the job involves, what skills it requires, and what conditions surround it.

Q79. Name the 8 uses/objectives of Job Analysis. A: Job redesign, work standards, recruitment, selection, training, performance appraisal, job evaluation, safety.

Q80. What is the Time Rate Wage System? A: A wage system where employees are paid based on the amount of time they work (hourly, daily, weekly, or monthly) regardless of output volume.

Q81. Name three ways HRM can help a firm gain sustainable competitive advantage. A: Maximize value added by employees; acquire rare employees; develop a culture that cannot be copied.

Marketing Management

Q82. Define Marketing Management. A: The process of planning and executing strategies for product, price, promotion, and distribution decisions to achieve customer satisfaction and business goals.

Q83. What are the 4 Ps of Marketing? A: Product, Price, Promotion, Place (Distribution).

Q84. What is the Production Concept? A: A marketing philosophy focused on mass production and low-cost manufacturing — assumes demand will absorb whatever is produced cheaply in large quantities.

Q85. What is "Marketing Myopia"? A: When a company is so focused on improving its product that it loses sight of actual customer needs and misses fundamental market shifts.

Q86. What is the core idea of the Marketing Concept? A: First understand customer needs, then build and deliver a product that meets those needs better than the competition.

Q87. What does the Societal Marketing Concept add to the Marketing Concept? A: It adds the requirement to consider society's long-term welfare alongside customer wants and company profits.

Q88. What are the four pillars of the Modern Marketing Concept? A: Customer-oriented approach, market research-driven decisions, long-term customer relationship focus, and value creation for the customer.

Q89. What is the main advantage of a Product-Based Marketing Organization? A: Each product line gets dedicated attention and expertise, allowing specialized marketing strategies for each product.

Q90. What is the key difference between Industrial Selling (B2B) and Consumer Selling (B2C) in terms of decision-making? A: B2B decisions are rational and technical, often involving a committee and long negotiation. B2C decisions mix emotion and reason, are made quickly, often by one individual.

Q91. Why do B2B salespersons need stronger technical knowledge than B2C salespersons? A: Because buyers in industrial selling are technical experts who ask detailed specification questions — a non-technical salesperson cannot answer credibly.

Q92. Define Advertising. A: Paid, non-personal promotion of a product or service through media channels to inform, persuade, remind, or build loyalty among a target audience.

Q93. State the four objectives of advertising in order. A: Inform → Persuade → Remind → Build Brand Loyalty.

Q94. What is the difference between B2B's "Target Customer Group" and B2C's "Market Segmentation"? A: B2B targets a finite, identifiable list of specific organizations. B2C divides the mass consumer population into segments by age, income, geography, or behavior.

Q95. Name four types of advertising. A: Traditional (TV, Radio, Newspaper), Digital (Social media, Search engines), Outdoor (Billboards), Direct Marketing (Email, SMS).

Q96. Name four elements of an advertising strategy. A: Target audience selection, message design, media selection, budget planning.

Q97. Name four modern challenges in advertising. A: High competition, changing customer behavior, digital media dominance, rising advertising costs.

Q98. What is the evolution order of the five marketing concepts? A: Production Concept → Product Concept → Selling Concept → Marketing Concept → Societal Marketing Concept.

Q99. What type of marketing organization would a bank use that has separate divisions for Corporate, Retail, and SME customers? A: Market-Based Organization (organized by customer segment).

Q100. What is the relationship between productivity and job satisfaction? A: They are interdependent — satisfied employees are more productive, and high productivity leads to recognition which increases satisfaction. Poor satisfaction leads to low productivity.
